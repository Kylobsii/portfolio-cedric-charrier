Suggested structure for each project page, so they stay consistent and easy to skim:

# Project Name
One-line pitch (the hook — what it is, in a single sentence).

**Role:** what you did
**Team size:** solo / N people
**Duration:** X weeks/months
**Tools:** engine, language, libraries
**Links:** [GitHub](...) · [Play it](...) · [Video](...)

## Overview
2-4 sentences of context — what the project is, why it exists.

## What I built
Bullet points of concrete contributions. Be specific: systems, features, tools —
not "worked on gameplay" but "implemented the dash/dodge system with i-frames and
a cooldown UI".

## Screenshots / Video
Embed images or a gameplay clip.

## Challenges & what I learned
1-2 concrete technical problems and how you solved them. This is the part
recruiters actually read closely — it shows problem-solving, not just output.
